DecHW: Second-Order Decentralized Federated Learning

A PyTorch-based decentralized training framework for peer-to-peer federated learning, with a Hessian-based aggregation method to address data and model heterogeneity.

Overview

DecHW addresses decentralized federated learning where clients collaborate directly over peer-to-peer networks without a central server. The system is designed for heterogeneous data distributions, heterogeneous model states, and constrained communication.

Problem

In decentralized federated learning, clients may have different data distributions, different model initialization states, and varying communication links. Standard averaging can lose important parameter-level information and slow convergence under non-IID settings.

What I Built

  • Built a PyTorch-based decentralized FL training environment.
  • Implemented peer-to-peer model exchange over graph-based communication topologies.
  • Developed DecHW, a Hessian-based aggregation method for parameter-level weighting.
  • Used second-order information to capture local parameter importance.
  • Benchmarked against standard FL baselines including FedAvg-style aggregation.

Tech Stack

Python, PyTorch, Federated Learning, Distributed Training, Graph Networks, Non-IID Data, CNNs.

Impact

DecHW improves convergence speed and communication efficiency under heterogeneous, non-IID decentralized settings by using parameter-level importance during neighborhood aggregation.
